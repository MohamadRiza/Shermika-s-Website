Bridging Continents: How Cross-Marketing Promoted Tourism in Sri Lanka & Maldives

Dr. Shermika Kuweju CMO & Global Tourism Consultant

For years, tropical destinations like Sri Lanka and the Maldives operated in geographical isolation, marketing their beaches independently. However, in the modern landscape of global aviation, the most successful campaigns are built on alliances. By bridging airline capacity with targeted B2B cross-marketing, we unlocked unprecedented inbound tourist yields.

During my tenure heading sales and marketing in the region, one of our key strategic missions was expanding Sri Lanka and the Maldives' visibility across Europe and the United States. Rather than waiting for travelers to seek out our shores, we proactively brought the destinations to the decision-makers through structured cross-marketing partnerships.

The Power of B2B DMC Workshops

To establish Turkish Airlines' presence in the regional market, we coordinated directly with local Destination Management Companies (DMCs). We realized that travel operators in cities like London, Frankfurt, Paris, and New York were eager to book high-end clients but lacked direct relationships with local hoteliers and transport networks.

We designed a series of international workshops, offering local DMCs direct platforms to present their travel products to European and American tour operators. By partnering with global air carriers, we subsidized transport expenses, finalized corporate sales agreements, and created packages that combined transit flights with seamless local transfers.

"Double-country promotions allow long-haul travelers to experience two distinct worlds in one holiday: the deep cultural heritage of Sri Lanka, and the ultimate pristine luxury of the Maldives."

Implementing Twin-Destination Promotions

One of our most innovative campaigns was the "Double-Country Promotion" targeting the United States market. Recognizing that American travelers flying halfway across the world want maximum variety, we packaged Sri Lanka and the Maldives as a single, complementary itinerary.

Travelers could spend five days exploring the ancient temple fortresses and lush tea hills of Sri Lanka before taking a short 1-hour flight to spend five days in a private overwater bungalow in the Maldives. Crucially, we secured regulatory fifth-freedom traffic rights for Turkish Airlines between Colombo and Male, enabling us to transport passengers between these countries under a single airline ticket. This drastically simplified ticketing, lowered flight costs, and increased regional passenger traffic by over 80%.

Embassy & Ambassadorial Alliances

Tourism promotion doesn't succeed in a vacuum; it requires political and diplomatic backing. We worked closely with Sri Lankan and Maldivian embassies across the globe to run exclusive tourism promotional events. These events invited luxury travel journalists, corporate planners, and airline representatives to cultural presentations, building premium networks that sustained regional yields even during challenging economic seasons.

Ultimately, cross-marketing proved that cooperation outperforms competition. By linking airline networks, local operators, and diplomatic channels, we turned Sri Lanka and the Maldives into highly accessible, world-famous tropical corridors.
